Progress

Thanks to our sponsors we have acquired the sensors and vehicles that we need to create our first autonomous vehicle.

We believe that we have a unique approach to stereo correspondence which enables us to use cheaper sensors than other autonomous cars (like the Google car) currently use.

All of our work is freely available under the BSD license - you can view our source code on our GitHub page.

June 2015

DriveAI Platform

This month we are focusing on testing our alpha release of perception and actuating our first vehicle. We are building a software package that will integrate several sensors, motors, and processors and eventually culminate in a basic self driving car. We're currently focusing on the portion of the platform that informs the car what the surrounding looks like and interprets the data. We call this "Perception". With simulated data, we're developing our "Seeded Stereo Correspondence" procedure. You can find the details of this procedure Here
